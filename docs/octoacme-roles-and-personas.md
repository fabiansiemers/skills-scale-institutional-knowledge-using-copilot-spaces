# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure features are user-focused, accessible, and intuitive. They collaborate with Product Managers, Developers, and stakeholders to validate usability and design coherent user experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Design and validate user interfaces for usability and accessibility
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Participate in backlog refinement to assess usability implications
- Review and test deliverables before release to ensure design fidelity
- Maintain design systems and accessibility standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Ensure consistency across product surfaces
- Validate designs with real user feedback
- Minimize usability issues in production

### Typical Communication
- Weekly syncs with Product Managers and Developers
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Acceptance criteria collaboration during backlog refinement
- Usability test findings and recommendations

---

## Scrum Masters / Agile Coaches

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches facilitate agile ceremonies, shield teams from distractions, and foster continuous improvement. They ensure teams follow agile principles and help resolve impediments.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Coach team members on agile practices and principles
- Identify and help remove impediments and blockers
- Foster psychological safety and team collaboration
- Track team health metrics and velocity trends
- Coordinate with Project Managers on delivery timelines

### Goals
- Maximize team effectiveness and autonomy
- Ensure agile principles are understood and applied
- Drive continuous improvement through retrospectives
- Protect team focus and reduce context switching

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members on process improvements
- Coordination with PM on risk and dependency management
- Retrospective action items and follow-up

---

## Technical Architects

### Role Summary
Technical Architects provide technical direction, document system design, and ensure architectural decisions align with scalability, maintainability, and security standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define and document system architecture and technical standards
- Review technical approaches and design proposals
- Ensure architectural decisions support scalability and performance
- Guide technical discussions and resolve architectural conflicts
- Evaluate new technologies and propose architectural improvements
- Coordinate with Developers and Project Managers on technical timelines

### Goals
- Maintain system quality, scalability, and security
- Minimize technical debt and architectural drift
- Enable developers with clear technical guidance
- Ensure architectural decisions support business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review boards and technical design reviews
- Documentation in architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Technical guidance in PR reviews
- Coordination with PM and developers on technical feasibility

---

## Customer/End-User Advocates

### Role Summary
Customer/End-User Advocates represent end-user needs throughout the project lifecycle. They surface real user pain points and provide actionable feedback to ensure deliverables meet user expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize user feedback and feature requests
- Represent user perspective in planning and review sessions
- Participate in sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Validate features from an end-user perspective
- Identify gaps between user needs and planned features
- Communicate user impact to Product Managers and stakeholders

### Goals
- Ensure user voice is heard in decision-making
- Reduce user-facing issues through early feedback
- Improve product-market fit and user satisfaction
- Bridge gap between internal assumptions and user reality

### Typical Communication
- User feedback summaries and pain point analysis
- Participation in planning and retrospectives
- Feature validation and acceptance testing
- Direct communication with Product Managers and UX Designers

---

## Data Analysts

### Role Summary
Data Analysts enable data-driven decision-making by measuring project success, tracking key metrics, and providing insights that inform planning and retrospectives.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Design and implement experiments to validate hypotheses
- Report on project metrics (velocity, adoption, usage, quality)
- Analyze retrospective data to identify improvement opportunities
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Collaborate with Product Managers and teams on success criteria

### Goals
- Enable evidence-based decision-making
- Provide visibility into project health and outcomes
- Identify trends and opportunities for improvement
- Validate product hypotheses with real data

### Typical Communication
- Weekly/monthly metric reports and dashboards
- Experiment design and results sharing
- Data-driven insights in planning and retrospectives
- Collaboration with PM and PdM on success metrics

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- The expanded set of personas reflects the full range of contributors to project success, enabling better accountability, clearer onboarding, and more comprehensive process coverage.
